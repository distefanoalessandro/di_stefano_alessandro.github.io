Short Bio

Alessandro Di Stefano received his BSc (2009) and MSc degrees (2012) in Telecommunications Engineering from the Department of Electrical, Electronic and Computer Engineering (DIEEI) at University of Catania, Italy. He received the Ph.D. in Systems Engineering from University of Catania, in 2015. During the Ph.D., he attended several conferences on complex networks and he made various internships at Computer Laboratory, Department of Computer Science and Technology, University of Cambridge (UK), under the supervision of Prof. Pietro Li\`o, with whom he has been collaborating since 2012. After his PhD, he has worked as a post-doctoral researcher at DIEEI, University of Catania, then as a research associate and teaching assistant at King’s College London (KCL), Department of Engineering, London, UK. Currently, he is working as a research associate at Computer Laboratory, University of Cambridge (UK) under the supervision of Prof. Pietro Liò, with whom he has been collaborating since 2012. He has been appointed as Lecturer in Computer Science at Teesside University, Department of Computer Science and Information Systems, Middlesbrough, UK (from 1 September 2020). Moreover, he collaborates with several Universities in Europe and all over the world and he has also been the co-organizer and local chair of various research events at Computer Laboratory. He has an interdisciplinary approach to research which incorporates engineering and computer science methodologies applied to socio-technological systems. His research interests include evolutionary game theory, complex networks, human cooperation, social behaviours, homophily, mobile crowdsensing, decision-making models, epidemic spreading, ICT and machine learning.
